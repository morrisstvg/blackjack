Each row is one player starting hand against one dealer upcard. Win, push and loss probabilities assume you play perfect basic strategy from that point on. Click a column header to sort.

Win % is the chance the hand wins money under the best non-surrender play. Where surrender is marked as the best play, it beats even that line's expected value by losing only half the bet. EV is the average profit per unit wagered.

Methodology & sources: all probabilities and expected values are computed live by an exact expected-value engine (infinite-deck model, a standard close approximation of a 6-deck shoe) under the rules shown on the front page: dealer stands on soft 17, double on any two cards including after splits, split aces receive one card, no resplits, late surrender available, blackjack pays 3:2, values conditioned on the dealer not holding a natural. Results agree with standard published expected-value references for these rules, such as the Wizard of Odds infinite-deck expected value tables and Griffin's The Theory of Blackjack. Because every page queries the same engine, the numbers always agree with each other.
